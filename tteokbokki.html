TTEOKBOKKI

INGREDIENTS



  • 1 pound of tteok (Korean rice cakes)
  • 4 cups of water
  • 1/4 pound of fish cakes, sliced
  • 1/2 onion, sliced
  • 2 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 2 tablespoons of gochujang
    (Korean chili paste)
  • 1 tablespoon of soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon of sugar
  • 1 tablespoon of corn syrup
    (or honey)
  • 1 tablespoon of vegetable oil
  • 1 tablespoon of sesame oil
  • Green onions, sliced (for garnish)


INSTRUCTIONS



  • Soak the tteok in cold water for 30 minutes to soften.
  • In a pot, bring 4 cups of water to a boil. Add the sliced fish cakes, onion, and garlic, and simmer
    for 10 minutes.
  • Strain the fish cakes, onion, and garlic from the broth, and set aside.
  • In a pan, heat the vegetable oil over medium-high heat. Add the gochujang, soy sauce, sugar,
    and corn syrup (or honey), and stir-fry for 1-2 minutes until the sauce is well combined and fragrant.
  • Add the strained fish cakes, onion, and garlic to the pan with the sauce, and stir-fry for another 2-3 minutes.
  • Add the tteok to the pan, and stir to coat the tteok with the sauce.
  • Add 1 cup of the fish cake broth to the pan, and stir to combine.
  • Cover the pan, and cook for 5-7 minutes over medium heat, stirring occasionally, until the tteok is
    fully cooked and the sauce has thickened.
  • Add the sesame oil to the pan, and stir to combine.
  • Garnish with sliced green onions, and serve hot.
