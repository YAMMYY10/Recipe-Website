MA PO TOFU

INGREDIENTS



  • 1 block of firm tofu (around 14 oz)
  • 2 tbsp vegetable oil
  • 1 tbsp Szechuan peppercorns
  • 1 tbsp fermented black beans, rinsed and minced
  • 3 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 2 tbsp ginger, minced
  • 1 lb ground pork
  • 2 tbsp chili bean paste (doubanjiang)
  • 1 tbsp soy sauce
  • 1 tbsp cornstarch
  • 1/2 cup chicken or vegetable broth
  • 2 green onions, thinly sliced


INSTRUCTIONS



  • Cut the tofu into small cubes and set aside.
  • Heat the vegetable oil in a wok or large skillet over medium-high heat.
  • Add the Szechuan peppercorns and stir-fry for about 30 seconds, until fragrant.
  • Add the minced black beans, garlic, and ginger, and stir-fry for another 30 seconds.
  • Add the ground pork and cook until browned, breaking it up with a spatula as it cooks.
  • Add the chili bean paste and stir-fry for a minute or so.
  • In a small bowl, mix together the soy sauce and cornstarch until smooth.
  • Add the chicken or vegetable broth to the wok and bring to a simmer.
  • Add the tofu cubes and gently stir to combine.
  • Pour the soy sauce and cornstarch mixture into the wok, stirring gently to thicken the sauce.
  • Simmer for a couple of minutes until the sauce has thickened and the tofu is heated through.
  • Garnish with sliced green onions and serve hot with steamed rice.
  • Enjoy your delicious Mapo Tofu!
