BIBIMBAP

INGREDIENTS



  • 1 cup of short-grain rice
  • 2 cups of water
  • 1 pound of beef, thinly sliced
  • 2 tablespoons of soy sauce
  • 1 tablespoon of sesame oil
  • 1 tablespoon of sugar
  • 1 tablespoon of minced garlic
  • 1 tablespoon of vegetable oil
  • 4 cups of assorted vegetables (spinach, bean sprouts, carrots,
    zucchini, mushrooms, etc.)
  • 4 eggs
  • 4 tablespoons of gochujang
    (Korean red pepper paste)
  • Sesame seeds for garnish


INSTRUCTIONS



  • Rinse the rice in cold water and drain. Combine the rice and water in a medium saucepan and bring
    to a boil. Reduce the heat to low, cover, and
    simmer for 15-20 minutes, or until the rice is tender and the water has been absorbed.
  • While the rice is cooking, marinate the beef in a mixture of soy sauce, sesame oil, sugar, and garlic
    for at least 30 minutes.
  • Heat the vegetable oil in a large skillet over medium-high heat. Add the beef and cook for 3-4 minutes,
    or until browned and cooked through. Remove from the skillet and set aside.
  • Add the assorted vegetables to the same skillet and stir-fry for 3-4 minutes, or until crisp-tender.
    Remove from the skillet and set aside.
  • In a separate skillet, fry the eggs over easy.
  • To assemble the bibimbap, divide the cooked rice into 4 bowls. Arrange the cooked beef and vegetables
    on top of the rice. Place a fried egg on top of each bowl.
  • Serve with a tablespoon of gochujang on top of each bowl and sprinkle with sesame seeds.
  • Stir everything together before eating.
