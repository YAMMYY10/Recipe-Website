SAUERBRATEN

INGREDIENTS



  • 3-4 pound beef roast, such as bottom round or chuck
  • 1 cup red wine vinegar
  • 1 cup water
  • 1 cup red wine
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tablespoon juniper berries
  • 1 tablespoon whole black
    peppercorns
  • 1 tablespoon whole allspice berries
  • 2 bay leaves
  • 1 tablespoon salt
  • 1/4 cup vegetable oil
  • 1/4 cup all-purpose flour
  • 1/2 cup water
  • 1/2 cup sour cream


INSTRUCTIONS



  • In a large bowl, mix together the red wine vinegar, water, red wine, chopped onion, minced garlic,
    juniper berries, black peppercorns, allspice berries, bay leaves, and salt.
  • Add the beef roast to the bowl and turn to coat it in the marinade.
  • Cover the bowl with plastic wrap and refrigerate for at least 24 hours, turning the beef roast
    occasionally.
  • Remove the beef roast from the marinade and pat it dry with paper towels. Reserve the marinade.
  • In a large Dutch oven or heavy pot, heat the vegetable oil over medium-high heat.
  • Once hot, add the beef roast and brown it on all sides.
  • Remove the beef roast from the pot and set it aside.
  • Add the flour to the pot and stir it into the oil to make a roux.
  • Cook the roux for 1-2 minutes, stirring constantly.
  • Gradually pour in the reserved marinade and 1/2 cup of water, stirring constantly to prevent lumps.
  • Return the beef roast to the pot and bring the mixture to a simmer.
  • Cover the pot and transfer it to a preheated 325Â°F oven.
  • Cook the beef roast for 2 1/2 to 3 hours, or until it is fork-tender.
  • Remove the beef roast from the pot and transfer it to a cutting board.
  • Strain the cooking liquid through a fine-mesh sieve and return it to the pot.
  • Bring the cooking liquid to a simmer and stir in the sour cream.
  • Cook the sauce for 1-2 minutes, or until it is heated through.
  • Slice the beef roast and serve it with the sauce on top.
  • Serve and enjoy!.
