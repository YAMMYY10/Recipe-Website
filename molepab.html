MOLE POBLANO

INGREDIENTS



  • 2-3 lbs. boneless, skinless chicken
    breasts or thighs
  • 6 dried ancho chilies, stemmed and seeded
  • 2 dried pasilla chilies, stemmed and seeded
  • 1/2 onion, chopped
  • 2 garlic cloves, chopped
  • 2 tomatoes, chopped
  • 1/4 cup raisins
  • 1/4 cup almonds
  • 1/4 cup sesame seeds
  • 1/4 cup unsweetened cocoa powder
  • 1 tsp. ground cinnamon
  • 1/4 tsp. ground cloves
  • 1/4 tsp. ground coriander
  • 1/4 tsp. ground anise
  • 1/4 tsp. ground cumin
  • 4 cups chicken broth
  • Salt, to taste
  • 2 tbsp. vegetable oil


INSTRUCTIONS



  • Preheat the oven to 350Â°F.
  • Toast the ancho and pasilla chilies on a baking sheet in the oven for about 5 minutes, until fragrant.
  • In a large pot, bring 4 cups of water to a boil. Add the toasted chilies and turn off the heat. Let the chilies soak
    in the hot water for about 30 minutes, until they are soft.
  • Drain the chilies and transfer them to a blender or food processor. Add the onion, garlic, tomatoes, raisins, almonds, sesame seeds, cocoa powder, cinnamon, cloves, coriander, anise, cumin, and chicken broth. Blend until smooth.
  • Heat the vegetable oil in a large skillet over medium-high heat. Add the chicken and cook until browned on both sides, about 5 minutes per side.
  • Pour the mole sauce over the chicken in the skillet. Reduce the heat to low and let the chicken simmer in the sauce for about 30 minutes, until the chicken is cooked through and the sauce has thickened. Stir occasionally to prevent sticking.
  • Season with salt to taste.
  • Serve hot with rice or tortillas, and garnish with sesame seeds or chopped cilantro if desired.
