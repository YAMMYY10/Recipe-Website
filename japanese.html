JAPANESE CUISINE


Sushi

Sushi is a traditional Japanese dish consisting of vinegared rice, often seasoned with sugar and salt, combined with raw or cooked seafood, vegetables, or other ingredients such as egg, tofu, or avocado.




Tempura

Tempura is a popular Japanese dish that consists of seafood or vegetables that are lightly battered and then deep-fried until crispy served with a dipping sauce made from soy sauce, mirin, and dashi, a Japanese broth made from dried fish and seaweed.




Sashimi

Sashimi is a Japanese dish that consists of thin slices of raw fish or seafood served without rice. The high-quality fish is sliced into thin, bite-sized pieces, varieties including tuna, salmon, yellowtail, and mackerel,etc.




Soba Noodles

Soba noodles are a type of Japanese noodle made from buckwheat flour and wheat flour. They are thin and have a slightly chewy texture, and are often served cold with a dipping sauce or hot in a soup.




Onigiri

Onigiri is a Japanese dish that consists of balls or triangles of rice that are often filled with a savory filling, such as grilled salmon, tuna mayonnaise, pickled plum, or seaweed. Onigiri is a popular snack or lunch item in Japan and is often wrapped in nori seaweed.
