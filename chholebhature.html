CHHOLE BHATURE

INGREDIENTS



    For the Chhole:

  • 2 cups chickpeas, soaked overnight
  • 2 tbsp oil
  • 1 tsp cumin seeds
  • 1 tsp coriander seeds
  • 1 onion, finely chopped
  • 1 tomato, finely chopped
  • 1 tsp ginger-garlic paste
  • 1 tsp red chili powder
  • 1 tsp cumin powder
  • 1 tsp coriander powder
  • 1/2 tsp turmeric powder
  • 1 tsp garam masala powder
  • Salt to taste
  • 2 cups water
  • 2 tbsp chopped coriander leaves

    • For the Bhature:

  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 tsp baking powder
  • 1/4 tsp baking soda
  • 1/4 tsp salt
  • 1/2 cup yogurt
  • 2 tbsp oil
  • Water as needed
  • Oil for frying


INSTRUCTIONS



    For the Chhole:

  • Drain the soaked chickpeas and rinse them well.
  • In a pressure cooker, add the soaked chickpeas, 2 cups of water, and salt to taste.
  • Pressure cook for 3-4 whistles or until the chickpeas are cooked.
  • In a separate pan, heat oil and add cumin seeds and coriander seeds. Sauté for a few seconds until fragrant.
  • Add finely chopped onions and sauté until they turn golden brown.
  • Add ginger-garlic paste and sauté for another minute.
  • Add finely chopped tomatoes and sauté until they turn soft and mushy.
  • Add red chili powder, cumin powder, coriander powder, turmeric powder, garam masala powder, and salt
    to taste. Mix well.
  • Add the cooked chickpeas along with the water and mix well.
  • Simmer for 10-15 minutes or until the chhole thickens.
  • Garnish with chopped coriander leaves and keep aside.

    • For the Bhature:

  • In a mixing bowl, add all-purpose flour, baking powder, baking soda, and salt. Mix well.
  • Add yogurt and oil and mix well to form a crumbly mixture.
  • Gradually add water and knead to form a soft and smooth dough.
  • Cover the dough and keep it aside for 30 minutes.
  • Divide the dough into equal-sized balls and roll them out into round discs.
  • Heat oil in a deep frying pan over medium heat.
  • Gently slide the rolled-out bhature into the hot oil and fry until it turns golden brown.
  • Drain on a paper towel to remove excess oil.
  • Serve hot Chhole with Bhature along with chopped onions and achaar (pickle) on the side. Enjoy!
