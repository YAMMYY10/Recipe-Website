GOLABKI

INGREDIENTS



  • 1 head of cabbage
  • 1 lb ground beef
  • 1 cup cooked white rice
  • 1 onion, chopped
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 egg, beaten
  • 1 tbsp dried parsley
  • 1 tsp salt
  • 1/2 tsp black pepper
  • 1 can (14 oz) tomato sauce
  • 1/4 cup brown sugar
  • 1/4 cup lemon juice
  • 1 cup water


INSTRUCTIONS



  • Preheat the oven to 350Â°F.
  • Bring a large pot of salted water to a boil.
  • Remove the outer leaves of the cabbage and place the head in the boiling water for 5-10 minutes,
    or until the leaves are soft and pliable.
  • Remove the cabbage from the water and let it cool.
  • Meanwhile, in a large bowl, mix together the ground beef, cooked rice, onion, garlic, egg, parsley,
    salt, and pepper.
  • Once the cabbage is cool enough to handle, carefully remove the leaves from the head and trim
    the tough stems from the bottom of each leaf.
  • Place a spoonful of the beef mixture onto the center of each cabbage leaf, fold the sides of the leaf
    over the filling, and roll up tightly.
  • Place the cabbage rolls seam-side down in a large baking dish.
  • In a separate bowl, whisk together the tomato sauce, brown sugar, lemon juice, and water.
  • Pour the sauce over the cabbage rolls, making sure they are completely covered.
  • Cover the baking dish with foil and bake for 1 hour.
  • Remove the foil and bake for an additional 15-20 minutes, or until the cabbage rolls are fully cooked
    and the sauce has thickened.
  • Serve hot and enjoy your homemade Polish Golabki!
