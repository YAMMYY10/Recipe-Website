NASI GORENG

INGREDIENTS



  • 3 cups cooked rice, preferably refrigerated for at least 2 hours or overnight
  • 2 tbsp oil (vegetable or canola)
  • 1 small onion, diced
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1/2 cup diced cooked chicken,
    shrimp, or tofu
  • 1 egg
  • 1/2 cup diced vegetables (such as carrots, peas, and green onions)
  • 1-2 tbsp kecap manis (sweet soy sauce)
  • 1-2 tsp sambal oelek (chili paste)
  • Additional sliced green onions and crispy fried shallots for garnish (optional)
  • You can also add other ingredients such as sliced mushrooms, diced bell peppers, or sliced chili peppers to spice things up.


INSTRUCTIONS



  • Cook rice according to package instructions, then refrigerate for at least 2 hours to overnight.
  • Heat oil in a wok or large frying pan over medium-high heat.
  • Add diced onion and cook until softened, about 2-3 minutes.
  • Add minced garlic and cook until fragrant, about 30 seconds.
  • Add diced chicken, shrimp, or tofu and cook until browned, about 3-4 minutes.
  • Push the ingredients to one side of the wok/pan and crack an egg into the empty space.
  • Scramble the egg until cooked, then mix it into the other ingredients.
  • Add diced vegetables such as carrots, peas, and green onions and cook until tender, about 2-3 minutes.
  • Add cold, cooked rice and stir-fry until heated through, about 5-6 minutes.
  • Add kecap manis (sweet soy sauce) and sambal oelek (chili paste) to taste, stirring to combine.
  • Garnish with additional sliced green onions and crispy fried shallots, if desired.
  • You can substitute kecap manis with a mixture of soy sauce and brown sugar.
