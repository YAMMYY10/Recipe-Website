MOQUECA

INGREDIENTS



  • 1 1/2 lbs. firm white fish fillets (such as cod or halibut), cut into chunks
  • 1 green bell pepper, sliced
  • 1 red bell pepper, sliced
  • 1 onion, sliced
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 can (14 oz.) diced tomatoes
  • 1 can (14 oz.) coconut milk
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil
  • 1 tablespoon palm oil (or additional olive oil)
  • Juice of 1 lime
  • Salt and black pepper to taste
  • Cilantro, chopped, for garnish


INSTRUCTIONS



  • Heat the olive oil and palm oil in a large pot or Dutch oven over medium heat.
  • Add the sliced bell peppers and onion and cook until softened, about 5-7 minutes.
  • Add the minced garlic and cook for another minute, until fragrant.
  • Add the diced tomatoes (with their juices) and coconut milk to the pot and stir to combine.
  • Bring the mixture to a simmer and let cook for 10-15 minutes, until slightly thickened.
  • Add the fish chunks to the pot and gently stir to coat with the sauce.
  • Season with salt and black pepper to taste.
  • Cover the pot and let cook for 10-15 minutes, until the fish is cooked through and flakes easily with a fork.
  • Squeeze the lime juice over the top of the Moqueca and sprinkle with chopped cilantro.
  • Serve hot with rice and/or crusty bread.
