BIRYANI

INGREDIENTS



    For the rice:

  • 2 cups Basmati rice
  • 4 cups water
  • 2 bay leaves
  • 2-3 cloves
  • 1 cinnamon stick
  • 1 tsp cumin seeds
  • Salt to taste

    • For the chicken:

  • 500 gm chicken, cut into pieces
  • 1 cup yogurt
  • 1 onion, finely chopped
  • 2 tomatoes, finely chopped
  • 2 tbsp ginger-garlic paste
  • 1 tsp cumin powder
  • 1 tsp coriander powder
  • 1 tsp red chili powder
  • 1/2 tsp turmeric powder
  • 1 tsp garam masala powder
  • Salt to taste
  • 2 tbsp oil
  • 1/2 cup mint leaves, finely chopped
  • 1/2 cup coriander leaves, finely chopped

    • For layering:

  • 2 onions, thinly sliced
  • 1/2 cup milk
  • A pinch of saffron
  • 2 tbsp ghee
  • 2 tbsp fried onions (optional)


INSTRUCTIONS



    For the rice:

  • Rinse the Basmati rice and soak it in water for 30 minutes.
  • In a pot, boil 4 cups of water with bay leaves, cloves, cinnamon stick, cumin seeds, and salt.
  • Drain the soaked rice and add it to the boiling water.
  • Cook until the rice is 70-80% done. Drain the water and keep the rice aside.

    • For the chicken:

  • Heat oil in a pan and add chopped onions. Sauté until the onions turn golden brown.
  • Add ginger-garlic paste and sauté for a few seconds.
  • Add chopped tomatoes and sauté until they turn soft and mushy.
  • Add cumin powder, coriander powder, red chili powder, turmeric powder, garam masala powder, and salt
    to taste. Mix well.
  • Add the chicken pieces and mix well.
  • Add yogurt and mix well. Cover and cook on low flame until the chicken is cooked and tender.
  • Add chopped mint leaves and coriander leaves and mix well.

    • For layering:

  • In a pan, heat ghee and add thinly sliced onions. Fry until the onions turn golden brown.
  • Soak saffron in warm milk.
  • Take a heavy-bottomed pot and spread a layer of the cooked rice at the bottom.
  • Spread a layer of the chicken mixture on top of the rice.
  • Add a layer of fried onions on top of the chicken mixture.
  • Repeat the layers of rice and chicken mixture until all the ingredients are used up.
  • Pour saffron milk over the top layer of rice.
  • Sprinkle fried onions on top (optional).
  • Cover the pot with a lid and cook on low flame for 10-15 minutes until the biryani is steaming hot.
  • Serve hot biryani with raita and salad. Enjoy your delicious Indian Biryani!
