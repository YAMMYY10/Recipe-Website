BRAZILIAN CUISINE


Feijoada

Considered by many as Brazil's national dish, it's a hearty stew made with black beans and various cuts of pork, beef, and sausage, and typically served with rice, collard greens, and orange slices.




Pão de queijo

Translating to "cheese bread," this snack is made from cassava flour, cheese, eggs, and milk, and is baked into small, puffy balls that are crispy on the outside and chewy on the inside.




Moqueca

A traditional seafood stew made with fish or shrimp, coconut milk, peppers, tomatoes, onions, garlic, and dendê oil, which gives it a distinct flavor and bright yellow color.




Acarajé

Acarajé, a popular street food is a deep-fried fritter made from black-eyed peas, onions, and salt, and typically served split open and stuffed with a spicy paste made from shrimp, palm oil, peppers, and other seasonings.




Farofa

Farofa is a traditional Brazilian side dish made from toasted manioc flour (also known as cassava flour), which is then mixed with various ingredients, such as onions, garlic, bacon, sausage, eggs, and spices.
