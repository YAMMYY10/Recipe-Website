GOI CUON

INGREDIENTS



  • 12 rice paper wrappers
  • 12 large cooked shrimp, peeled
    and deveined
  • 1 small head of lettuce, chopped
  • 1 small carrot, julienned
  • 1 small cucumber, julienned
  • 1/4 cup of fresh mint leaves
  • 1/4 cup of fresh cilantro leaves
  • 1/4 cup of fresh Thai basil leaves (optional)
  • 1/4 cup of chopped roasted peanuts

    • For the dipping sauce:

  • 2 tbsp. of hoisin sauce
  • 2 tbsp. of peanut butter
  • 1 tbsp. of soy sauce
  • 1 tbsp. of lime juice
  • 1 clove of garlic, minced
  • 1/4 tsp. of red pepper flakes
    (optional)
  • 3-4 tbsp. of water


INSTRUCTIONS



  • Fill a large bowl with warm water. Dip one rice paper wrapper in the water for 10-15 seconds until
    it becomes pliable. Place the wrapper on a flat surface.
  • Place 1 shrimp in the center of the wrapper. Add a small handful of chopped lettuce, carrot,
    cucumber, mint, cilantro, and Thai basil leaves (if using) on top of the shrimp.
  • Sprinkle 1 teaspoon of chopped peanuts on top of the filling.
  • Fold the bottom half of the wrapper over the filling. Fold the sides of the wrapper inward. Roll the wrapper tightly to enclose the filling completely. Repeat with the remaining ingredients to make more
    goi cuon.
  • In a small bowl, whisk together the hoisin sauce, peanut butter, soy sauce, lime juice, minced garlic,
    red pepper flakes (if using), and 3-4 tablespoons of water to make the dipping sauce. Add more water as
    needed to thin the sauce to your desired consistency.
  • Serve the goi cuon with the dipping sauce on the side.
