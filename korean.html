KOREAN CUISINE


Kimchi

Kimchi is a traditional Korean dish made with fermented vegetables, most commonly napa cabbage or Korean radish, and a mixture of seasonings including chili powder, garlic, ginger, and fish sauce. Kimchi is a staple in Korean cuisine and is served as a side dish with most meals.




Bulgogi

A popular Korean dish made with thin slices of marinated beef, grilled or stir-fried with vegetables and served with rice.




Bibimbap

A Korean rice bowl dish topped with vegetables, meat or tofu, and a fried egg. The ingredients are mixed together with a spicy sauce called gochujang before eating.




Tteokbokki

Tteokbokki is a popular Korean snack or street food made with chewy cylindrical-shaped rice cakes, called tteok, that are simmered in a spicy red sauce made with gochujang (red pepper paste) and other seasonings. It is often served with fish cakes, boiled eggs, and scallions.




Jajangmyeon

Jajangmyeon is a popular Korean-Chinese noodle dish made with a thick black bean sauce, called jajang, mixed with diced pork, onions, and other vegetables such as zucchini and potatoes. The sauce is usually made from a paste made with caramelized black bean powder, soy sauce, and sugar, among other ingredients.
