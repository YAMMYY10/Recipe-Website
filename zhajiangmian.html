ZHAJIANGMIAN

INGREDIENTS



    For the noodles:

  • 8 oz Chinese wheat noodles
  • Water for boiling

    • For the sauce:

  • 1/2 lb ground pork
  • 2 tbsp vegetable oil
  • 1 onion, diced
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1/2 cup diced carrots
  • 1/2 cup diced zucchini
  • 1/2 cup diced eggplant
  • 1/2 cup diced potato
  • 1/4 cup Zhajiang sauce (fermented soybean paste)
  • 1/4 cup hoisin sauce
  • 2 tbsp soy sauce
  • 1 tbsp sugar
  • 1/4 cup water

    • For serving:

  • Sliced cucumbers
  • Thinly sliced scallions
  • Chili oil (optional)

    • Note: Zhajiang sauce is a type of fermented soybean paste used in Chinese cooking. It can be found at most Asian grocery stores or can be made at home using various recipes available online.



INSTRUCTIONS



  • Cook the noodles according to package instructions until they are al dente. Drain and rinse under cold water to stop the cooking process. Set aside.
  • In a large pan or wok, heat 2 tablespoons of vegetable oil over medium-high heat. Add the diced onions and cook until they are translucent.
  • Add the minced garlic and cook for another 30 seconds until fragrant.
  • Add the ground pork and cook until browned, breaking it up with a spatula as it cooks.
  • Add the diced carrots, zucchini, eggplant, and potato to the pan and stir-fry for a few minutes until they begin to soften.
  • Add the Zhajiang sauce, hoisin sauce, soy sauce, sugar, and water to the pan. Stir well to combine all the ingredients.
  • Reduce the heat to medium-low and let the sauce simmer for 15-20 minutes, stirring occasionally, until the vegetables are tender and the sauce has thickened.
  • To serve, place a serving of noodles in a bowl and ladle the sauce over the top.
  • Top with sliced cucumbers and scallions, and drizzle with chili oil if desired.
  • Enjoy your delicious Chinese Zhajiangmian!
