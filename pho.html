PHO

INGREDIENTS



  • 1 lb. beef bones
  • 1 onion, peeled and halved
  • 3-4-inch piece of ginger, sliced
    in half lengthwise
  • 2 cinnamon sticks
  • 4 star anise
  • 4-6 cloves
  • 1 tbsp. coriander seeds
  • 6 cups of water
  • 1-2 lbs. of beef, thinly sliced (use sirloin or flank steak)
  • 8 oz. of rice noodles
  • 1 cup of fresh bean sprouts
  • 1 cup of fresh Thai basil
  • 1 cup of fresh cilantro
  • 1 lime, cut into wedges
  • Hoisin sauce and Sriracha sauce,
    for serving


INSTRUCTIONS



  • Preheat oven to 425 degrees F. Place beef bones, onion, and ginger on a baking sheet and roast for
    20-30 minutes, until browned.
  • In a large pot, dry roast the cinnamon sticks, star anise, cloves, and coriander seeds for 1-2 minutes
    until fragrant. Add in the roasted beef bones, onion, and ginger, and 6 cups of water.
  • Bring the pot to a boil, then reduce heat and simmer for at least 2-3 hours, or until the broth is rich
    and flavorful.
  • Strain the broth and discard the solids.
  • Cook the rice noodles according to package instructions.
  • Arrange the cooked noodles and thinly sliced beef in serving bowls.
  • Top with hot broth.
  • Top with bean sprouts, Thai basil, cilantro, and a squeeze of lime juice.
  • Serve with hoisin sauce and Sriracha sauce on the side.
