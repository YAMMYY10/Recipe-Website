SATAY

INGREDIENTS



  • 1 pound chicken breast, cut into
    small cubes
  • 1/4 cup kecap manis (sweet
    soy sauce)
  • 1 tbsp soy sauce
  • 1 tsp ground coriander
  • 1 tsp ground cumin
  • 1/2 tsp turmeric powder
  • 1/2 tsp salt
  • 1/4 tsp black pepper
  • 1 tbsp vegetable oil
  • 12-16 bamboo skewers, soaked in water for at least 30 minutes
  • Crushed peanuts and sliced
    cucumber for serving (optional)

    • For the peanut sauce:

  • 1 cup unsalted roasted peanuts
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 small onion, chopped
  • 1/4 cup kecap manis (sweet soy sauce)
  • 1 tbsp soy sauce
  • 1 tsp tamarind concentrate or lime juice
  • 1 tsp chili paste (such as sambal oelek)
  • 1 cup water


INSTRUCTIONS



  • In a mixing bowl, combine the chicken cubes with kecap manis, soy sauce, ground coriander, ground cumin, turmeric powder, salt, and black pepper. Stir to coat the chicken evenly and let it marinate for at least 1 hour, or up to 24 hours in the fridge.
  • Thread the chicken cubes onto the bamboo skewers, leaving some space between each piece.
  • Preheat a grill or grill pan over medium-high heat. Brush the skewers with vegetable oil and grill
    them for 8-10 minutes, turning occasionally, until the chicken is cooked through and slightly charred.
  • While the skewers are grilling, make the peanut sauce. In a blender or food processor, combine the peanuts, garlic, onion, kecap manis, soy sauce, tamarind concentrate or lime juice, chili paste, and water. Blend until smooth.
  • Transfer the peanut sauce to a small saucepan and heat over medium-low heat until warmed through, stirring occasionally.
  • Serve the satay skewers hot with the peanut sauce on the side, and optionally sprinkle some crushed peanuts and sliced cucumber over the top. Enjoy!
  • You can also use beef, pork, shrimp, or tofu instead of chicken for this recipe. Adjust the cooking time accordingly.
