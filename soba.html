SOBA NOODLES

INGREDIENTS



  • Soba noodles
  • Dipping sauce (e.g. tsuyu sauce, made from soy sauce, mirin, and dashi)
  • Soy sauce
  • Sesame oil
  • Rice vinegar
  • Sugar
  • Freshly ground black pepper
  • Toasted sesame seeds (I used a combination of white and black seeds)
  • Assorted toppings (e.g. scallions, grated daikon radish, wasabi, tempura flakes)


INSTRUCTIONS



  • Boil a large pot of water and add the soba noodles.
  • in the sauce mixture, if you like a spicier bite, add a pinch of red pepper flakes or Â¼ teaspoon of chili garlic
    sauce to the mix.
  • For the dip, to toast your sesame seeds, use a dry pan over medium high heat. Move the seeds around often in the pan to avoid burning.
  • Cook the noodles for 5-8 minutes, or until they are tender but still slightly firm. Drain the noodles and rinse under cold water to stop the cooking process.
  • Serve the soba noodles cold, with a dipping sauce on the side.
  • Add toppings of your choice, such as scallions, grated daikon radish, wasabi, or tempura flakes, to the dipping sauce or sprinkle over the noodles.
  • Enjoy your homemade Japanese soba noodles!
