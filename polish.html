POLISH CUISINE


Pierogi

These are dumplings that are typically filled with mashed potatoes, cheese, sauerkraut, or meat. They're boiled or fried and served with sour cream or fried onions.




Bigos

This is a traditional meat and cabbage stew that's often referred to as "Polish hunter's stew." It's made with a variety of meats, including pork, beef, and sausage, as well as cabbage, sauerkraut, and spices.




Rosół

Rosół is a traditional Polish soup that is often served as a first course or as a remedy for colds or flu. The soup is typically made with chicken, although sometimes beef or pork is used, and it's flavored with vegetables and herbs.




Golabki

These are cabbage rolls that are typically filled with a mixture of ground beef, rice, and onions. They're then cooked in a tomato sauce and served with mashed potatoes.




Zurek

This is a sour rye soup that's often served with sausage and hard-boiled eggs. It's made with a sourdough starter, which gives it its distinct sour taste, and is a popular dish in Poland, particularly during Easter.
