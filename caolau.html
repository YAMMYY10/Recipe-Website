CAO LAU

INGREDIENTS



  • 1 lb. of fresh cao lau noodles (or substitute with thick rice noodles)
  • 1/2 lb. of pork belly, thinly sliced
  • 2 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 1 tsp. of five-spice powder
  • 2 tbsp. of soy sauce
  • 1 tsp. of sugar
  • 1/2 tsp. of salt
  • 1/4 tsp. of black pepper
  • 4 cups of water
  • 1/2 cup of soybean sprouts
  • 1/2 cup of fresh herbs (such as cilantro and Thai basil)
  • Lime wedges, for serving


INSTRUCTIONS



  • In a large bowl, marinate the pork belly with garlic, five-spice powder, soy sauce, sugar, salt, and
    black pepper. Cover and refrigerate for at least 30 minutes or up to overnight.
  • Preheat the oven to 350Â°F. Place the marinated pork slices on a baking sheet and bake for 20-25
    minutes, or until the pork is cooked through and slightly caramelized.
  • Cook the cao lau noodles according to package instructions. Drain and rinse under cold water to stop
    the cooking process.
  • In a medium pot, bring 4 cups of water to a boil. Add the cooked noodles and soybean sprouts and
    let simmer for 1-2 minutes, until heated through.
  • Divide the noodle and sprout mixture into serving bowls. Top with the baked pork slices and fresh
    herbs.
  • Squeeze a lime wedge over each bowl and serve immediately.
